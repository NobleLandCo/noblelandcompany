## The 5 State Strategies (A/B Testing Framework)

Each state has a distinct emotional angle and SEO strategy. Every blog post written for a state must stay within its angle. This is how we A/B test what converts.

---

### 🟠 OKLAHOMA — "Stop the Bleed"
**Core angle:** Tax burden / cost of holding land
**Emotional trigger:** Frustration with wasted money
**Ideal seller persona:** Landowner paying taxes on land they don't use, possibly inherited
**Primary keywords:** sell land Oklahoma, sell vacant land Oklahoma, cash for land Oklahoma
**SEO differentiator:** ROI/loss framing — position holding land as actively costing money
**Tone:** Direct, pragmatic, no-nonsense
**CTAs:** "Stop paying taxes on land you don't need"
**Blog topics to use this angle:**
- Tax implications of holding vacant land in Oklahoma
- How much does vacant land actually cost per year in Oklahoma?
- Inherited land in Oklahoma: sell it or keep paying?
- Oklahoma county tax delinquency: what happens if you stop paying

---

### 🟢 KENTUCKY — "Family Land, Handled With Care"
**Core angle:** Emotional / legacy / inherited land
**Emotional trigger:** Family history, relief, respect
**Ideal seller persona:** Inherited land from a parent or grandparent, doesn't live in KY, feels guilty/overwhelmed
**Primary keywords:** sell inherited land Kentucky, family land Kentucky, we buy land Kentucky
**SEO differentiator:** Empathy + trust — position Noble Land Co. as the honest, respectful buyer
**Tone:** Warm, personal, conversational — like a trusted neighbor
**CTAs:** "Move forward on your terms"
**Blog topics to use this angle:**
- How to sell inherited land in Kentucky without probate headaches
- What to do with family land in Kentucky you don't want to keep
- Selling land in Kentucky after a parent passes away
- Kentucky heir property: what it is and how to sell it

---

### 🔵 WISCONSIN — "The Math Doesn't Lie"
**Core angle:** Financial / data-driven / ROI
**Emotional trigger:** Logic, FOMO, cost comparison
**Ideal seller persona:** Analytical landowner, someone who bought land as an investment, maybe a cabin that never got built
**Primary keywords:** sell vacant land Wisconsin, vacant land Wisconsin, sell land Wisconsin cash
**SEO differentiator:** Numbers + comparisons — cost tables, 10-year projections, hard data
**Tone:** Analytical, straightforward, facts-first
**CTAs:** "Run the numbers yourself"
**Blog topics to use this angle:**
- Wisconsin vacant land carrying costs: a 10-year breakdown
- Is vacant land in Wisconsin a good investment? (honest answer)
- How much are Wisconsin property taxes on vacant land?
- Wisconsin land values by county: what your land is actually worth

---

### 🔴 TENNESSEE — "Speed Closes Deals"
**Core angle:** Urgency / speed / market timing
**Emotional trigger:** FOMO on rising prices, anxiety about waiting too long
**Ideal seller persona:** Motivated seller — divorce, probate, financial pressure, or just impatient
**Primary keywords:** sell land fast Tennessee, sell land Tennessee, cash land buyer Tennessee
**SEO differentiator:** Speed framing — 14-day close, timeline tables, market urgency
**Tone:** Urgent, confident, action-oriented
**CTAs:** "Close in 14 days"
**Blog topics to use this angle:**
- How to sell land in Tennessee in 30 days or less
- Tennessee land market 2025: is now the time to sell?
- Sell land fast in Nashville, Knoxville, Chattanooga suburbs
- Delinquent land taxes in Tennessee: what happens and how to get out

---

### ⚫ NORTH CAROLINA — "Local Expertise, Better Offers"
**Core angle:** Trust / local knowledge / better than national buyers
**Emotional trigger:** Skepticism of big corporations, desire for fair treatment
**Ideal seller persona:** Has been lowballed before, researching options, skeptical but open

**SEO differentiator:** Local expertise positioning — beat national buyers with real NC knowledge
**Tone:** Credible, expert, confident but not arrogant

## Blog Post Template (use for every post)

```
SLUG: [state-keyword-slug]
TITLE: [Primary keyword — benefit or outcome]
META TITLE: [65 chars max — keyword + brand]
META DESCRIPTION: [155 chars max — keyword + CTA]
STATE: [state name]
PRIMARY KEYWORD: [exact match, use in H1 + first 100 words]
SECONDARY KEYWORDS: [2-3 related terms]
ANGLE: [which of the 5 strategies above]
WORD COUNT TARGET: 1,200–1,800 words
```

### Post Structure (every post):
1. **H1** — contains primary keyword exactly
2. **Intro (100-150 words)** — hook with the state angle, mention primary keyword in first sentence
3. **H2 — The Core Problem** — agitate the pain point specific to this state's angle
4. **H2 — Your Options** — present 3-4 options (always position Noble Land Co. last as the best solution)
5. **H2 — Why Noble Land Co.** — trust-builder section, tailor to state angle
6. **H2 — How It Works** — 3-step process (Contact → Offer → Close)
7. **H2 — FAQ** — 3-5 questions, include keywords naturally
8. **CTA Section** — clear call to action, link to state landing page

### Rules:
- Never stuff keywords. 1-2% density max.
- Each post must link internally to its state landing page
- Always mention specific counties or cities in the state (adds local SEO value)
- No fluff. Every paragraph must earn its place.
- Write at an 8th grade reading level — simple, clear, direct
- Each post must feel like it was written FOR that state's seller persona, not a generic template

## What NOT to Do
- Don't mix angles across states (don't write an emotional/family post for Wisconsin)
- Don't repeat the same H2 structure in every post — vary it
- Don't write generic content that could apply to any state — always make it state-specific
- Don't mention specific offer prices or guarantees
